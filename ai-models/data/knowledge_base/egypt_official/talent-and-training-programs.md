# Egypt Tech Talent Pool and Government Training Programs (Official)

## The talent pool

Egypt offers the largest and youngest multilingual talent pool in the Middle
East and the second largest in the EMEA region. Each year over 667,500 higher
education graduates from 82 universities join the workforce, and 37% of
graduates hold science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM)
degrees (source: ITIDA Talent Landscape,
itida.gov.eg/english/programs/talent-landscape). Around 80% of the working
population speaks English and other European languages, compared with 20–65%
in other leading offshore destinations (source: ITIDA Talent Landscape).
Per EY analysis cited by ITIDA, cost arbitrage for Egyptian talent is up to
60% versus other leading regional offshoring hubs.

## Government investment in digital skills

Egypt's training budget for digital skills rose fiftyfold over nine years,
and the number of trainees increased 150-fold — from 2,600 trainees with
EGP 32.5 million in 2014/2015 to 400,000 trainees with EGP 1.7 billion in
2022/2023 (source: ITIDA Talent Landscape).

## Free and subsidized programs an Egyptian job seeker can actually join

These are official MCIT/ITIDA capacity-building initiatives (sources: ITIDA
Skills Development program pages, itida.gov.eg; MCIT Digital Egypt Generations):

- **Digital Egypt Pioneers Initiative (DEPI)** — trains 90,000 university
  students in technology tracks under MCIT's Digital Egypt Generations.
- **Digital Egypt Builders Initiative (DEBI)** — a practical master's-level
  scholarship granting 1,000 specialists per cohort training in technological
  specializations.
- **FWD 2.0 (Future Work is Digital)** — scholarship offering technical
  training, career enablement and job opportunities to prepare young
  Egyptians for the digital workforce.
- **ITIDA Gigs Freelancing Program** — a 3-month structured program equipping
  youth with skills and mentorship to win paid freelance work on global
  platforms.
- **Train to Hire (ITS-BPS and Electronics tracks)** — specialized training
  qualifying job seekers to join offshoring companies, run in cooperation
  with hiring companies since 2017; the electronics track (Q4E) operates
  under the Egypt Makes Electronics initiative.
- **Summer Training** — trains computer science and engineering students on
  current technology during the summer vacation.
- **Up4Jobs** — ITIDA–GIZ initiative supporting German language skills for
  graduates seeking roles with German-market-focused IT/ITeS companies in
  Egypt.

## Why this matters for career planning

For an Egyptian learner, these programs are credible, low-cost routes into
the roles this platform covers (backend, frontend, data, DevOps, mobile,
UI/UX): they combine training with direct employer pipelines (Train to Hire,
Recruitment Initiative) and are backed by the same agencies reporting the
sector's job growth (source: ITIDA Skills Development and Industry Outlook
pages).
