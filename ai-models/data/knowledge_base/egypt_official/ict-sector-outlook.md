# Egypt ICT Sector Outlook (Official Figures)

## Sector growth and economic role

Egypt's ICT sector has been the country's highest-growing sector over the past
8 years, maintaining a sustained annual growth rate of 14–16% (source: ITIDA
Industry Outlook, itida.gov.eg/English/Programs/Industry-Outlook). Digital
exports have recorded 25% growth since 2022, and in 2025 Egypt's digital and
offshoring exports reached USD 4.8 billion, spanning IT services, business
process outsourcing, and engineering R&D (source: ITIDA press release "Egypt
Doubles Digital Offshoring Exports to 4.8B Dollars", itida.gov.eg).

## Job creation from offshoring agreements

In 2022, ITIDA signed agreements with 29 global companies committing to 34,000
new export-oriented ICT jobs; by the end of 2024, 60,000 jobs were delivered,
exceeding projections. At the Global Offshoring Summit in Cairo (November
2025), ITIDA signed 55 new agreements expected to create more than 75,000
additional jobs over the following three years — 39 companies expanding
existing Egyptian centers and 16 entering the Egyptian market for the first
time (source: ITIDA Industry Outlook).

## The employer landscape for Egyptian tech talent

Egypt hosts 240+ offshoring companies operating 270+ global service delivery
centres serving clients in over 100 countries across Europe, North America,
the GCC, Africa, and East Asia (source: ITIDA Industry Outlook). Services span
IT services (software development and testing, IT consulting, infrastructure
outsourcing), Business Process Services (customer experience, corporate and
financial functions), Knowledge Services, and Engineering R&D.

Engineering, Research & Development is an accelerating segment: global firms
including Valeo, Siemens, GlobalFoundries, STMicroelectronics, Luxoft, and
Goodix, alongside Egyptian players such as Si-Vision and BrightSkies, run
electronics design, embedded software, and systems engineering work from Egypt
(source: ITIDA Industry Outlook). Under the "Egypt Makes Electronics"
presidential initiative, four large manufacturers of mobile phones and tablets
(Vivo, Nokia, Xiaomi and Samsung) manufacture in Egypt with a combined
investment of EGP 2 billion and a production capacity of 20 million phones
(source: ITIDA, Egypt Makes Electronics program page).

## What this means for job seekers

The official sector trajectory — double-digit growth, tens of thousands of
committed export-oriented jobs, and 240+ international employers operating
locally — indicates sustained demand for software development, testing,
infrastructure, BPS, and embedded/semiconductor engineering skills in the
Egyptian market (sources: ITIDA Industry Outlook; MCIT ICT Indicators
publications, mcit.gov.eg/en/Publications).
